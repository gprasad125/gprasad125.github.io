Methodology

The data we were given was quite intense with regard to size and specificity of information, and differing comfort levels within our group in regard to a variety of data science techniques meant we had to bridge many gaps in order to get everyone on a level plane. Some of the tools and concepts our team used in this project were:

  • Data cleaning of datasets with more than 3 million rows (Pandas, Numpy)
  • Large-file storage and management (Git, Git LFS, etc.)
  • Data Visualization and Pattern Recognition (Matplotlib, Seaborn)
  • Data Modeling and Prediction (Sklearn, … ?)

Each of these were accompanied by their own unique issues and problems that the team had to grapple with and solve.

Issues

Although we were given some pretty neatly structured data, primarily in the form of JSON key-pair values, high levels of nesting and inconsistent variable presence made cleaning a unique challenge. Furthermore, compiling our many datasets (1000+ files) into one single CSV file yielded a 2GB output, meaning it was too large to push to GitHub, which was an issue that we were not expecting to work with this project.
